06 April 2029
                COBR, Parliament Annex / Hackney Wick / UCL Molecular Science Unit
FADE IN:
ONYXBONE: The Bone Protocol
EPISODE 3: Between Disclosure and Disinformation
As Naila's body rebels with chilling autonomy, the highest echelons of government struggle to define an enemy that rewrites biology and language, while a clandestine corporation orchestrates a disinformation campaign to control the fracturing narrative.
07:00 BST - Hackney Wick
INT. NAILA'S FLAT - KITCHEN - MORNING
NAILA (20s) is leaning over the kitchen sink. She's COUGHING, wracking her body, until her throat is raw and bleeding. Her face is pale, streaked with sweat.
When the fit subsides, she stares into the basin. Something sits in the water. SMALL, GREY, RIBBED. Not a tooth. Not a stone. It pulsed with a faint, internal light yesterday, before the dog incident. Now, it's just... inert. But still ominous.
She reaches for it, hesitant. Her fingers brush the water. A sudden, sharp SHIVER runs through her.
The small object hums once. A low, resonant sound, almost imperceptible to the ear, but Naila *feels* it in her teeth, in the hinge of her jaw.
Then, SILENCE. The hum stops. The object is just there.
She leaves it. She can't touch it. She can't look at it. It feels... like a demand.
CUT TO:
09:00 BST - COBR Briefing Room, Parliament Annex
INT. COBR BRIEFING ROOM - DAY
The room is sealed, stark, and humming with the low thrum of projector fans. Senior government and intelligence officials are gathered around a long table. SIR CHARLES AUSTEN (50s, Cabinet Secretary, sharp, tired) sits at the head.
On a large screen, a THERMAL SCAN of a patient's ribcage. The bone structure is distorted, displaying impossible, geometric patterns. A blurred-out MAN (40s, military intelligence, precise, clipped voice) gestures to the image.
MILITARY INTEL (V.O.)
...and as you can see, the calcification patterns are entirely inconsistent with any known biological anomaly. We believe the bone is now operating as a second-order logic substrate.
A collective intake of breath. No one laughs. A WOMAN from DHSC (Department of Health and Social Care) crosses herself under the table.
AUSTEN
'Second-order logic substrate'? Explain.
MILITARY INTEL
It's not just a symptom, Sir. It's a processor. The host skeleton is no longer merely supporting the body; it's interpreting, storing, and transmitting data.
DHSC OFFICIAL
So... it's a disease of the mind?
MILITARY INTEL
We are not fighting a disease. We are interpreting an emergence. The initial broadcast seeded interface logic directly into cortical patterns. That's why infected subjects are not contagious in a viral sense. They are recursive.
AUSTEN
(Nodding slowly) Right. What's the plan for public messaging? We can't have panic.
A graphic flashes on screen: "COBR BRIEFING - RED FOLDER - 06/04/2029". It lists key findings and recommendations.
(ON SCREEN: COBR INTERNAL MINUTES)
"KEY QUOTES: 'We are not fighting a disease. We are interpreting an emergence.' 'The broadcast seeded interface logic into cortical patterns.' 'Infected subjects are not contagious. They are recursive.' RECOMMENDATIONS: Tier 4 blackout over all research terms: 'calcite bloom,' 'bone OS,' 'marrow wave.' Media phrase suggestion: 'neurostructural mythos disorder.' Pre-emptive counter-conspiracy seeding on social media. NOTE: Foreign agencies showing signs of identical emergence vectors."
DISSOLVE TO:
11:30 BST - UCL Molecular Science Unit
INT. UCL LAB C7 - DAY
The lab is eerily quiet. A half-eaten sandwich sits on a desk, a coffee mug still warm. Files are scattered. DR. LIAM O'CONNELL (30s, agitated, junior researcher) walks through, shining a flashlight into the dark corners. He's accompanied by DETECTIVE INSPECTOR ANNA REID (40s, sharp, no-nonsense), who is examining a wiped workstation.
REID
No forced entry. No sign-out. Mobile signals cut simultaneously. Four researchers, vanished. What were they working on, Dr. O'Connell?
O'CONNELL
Bone conductivity. Bio-semantical interfaces. It was... niche. Dr. Beltran was the lead on Sample 47A. A strange artifact, delivered unlogged.
Reid pulls open a desk drawer. Inside, a crumpled piece of paper. Beltran's handwriting.
(ON SCREEN: BELTRAN'S NOTE)
"Don't let the bones finish thinking."
Reid looks up, disturbed. O'Connell approaches a cryo storage unit.
O'CONNELL
And Sample 47A... it's gone.
A small, hidden floor sensor on the lab floor flashes once, then dies. Reid didn't see it.
CUT TO:
13:00 BST - Aurelion Group HQ, Executive Boardroom
INT. AURELION GROUP - BOARDROOM - DAY
The mood is tense. DR. S. VEY (50s, Chair), KWAN (CDO), MENDEZ (PR), and DR. LENORA IQBAL (Chief Science Officer, now working for Aurelion) are present. Iqbal looks more composed than in the NHS meeting, though the dark circles under her eyes are pronounced.
VEY
UCL disappearances confirmed. Is there a connection to the 47A sample?
IQBAL
Highly probable. That sample was unique. 4cm oval, ribbed casing, self-cohering. It resisted melting. Emitted heat on proximity. Our internal analysis showed a crystalline layer with embedded glyphs. Not natural. Not noise.
KWAN
(Tapping her tablet) The pattern matches emerging signatures from our LAMI stack, Dr. Vey. This isn't just an anomaly. It's a designed intermediary. Someone put it there.
MENDEZ
(Flipping through notes) Regardless of origin, the narrative must hold. 'Marrow misalignment' is trending. The geometric dream reports are increasing. Sky News is prepping a segment. If the glyph coil footage from Tallinn airs again, we lose narrative control completely.
VEY
Then we preempt. Dr. Iqbal, your best guess on a public descriptor? Something... reassuringly medical, yet vague enough to absorb everything.
IQBAL
'Neurostructural Mythos Disorder' was suggested by COBR. But frankly, it sounds like something from a horror movie. My team drafted 'Somatic Integration Syndrome' for internal NHS use, but for wider public...
MENDEZ
(Interrupting) We need to avoid 'infection' or 'spread'. Use 'resonance adaptation'. Focus on 'brief episodes of wordless empathy or knowledge transfer'. 'A new interface is unfolding.' That's positive. Futuristic. Less terrifying.
Iqbal looks at Mendez, a flicker of disgust in her eyes, quickly masked.
(ON SCREEN: NHS COMMS DRAFT)
"SUBJECT: PUBLIC GUIDANCE - SYMPTOMS RESPONSE. DRAFT - NOT FOR CIRCULATION. OBJECTIVE: Create stable narrative for public to process unexplained symptoms: Bone discomfort or 'internal signal sensation'; Sleep loss with vivid geometric dreaming; Brief episodes of wordless empathy or knowledge transfer; Sudden phonemic echoing (repeating phrases heard only internally). WORKING DIRECTION: Avoid terms like 'infection' or 'spread'. Use 'resonance adaptation'. PHRASE TESTED POSITIVE: 'A new interface is unfolding.' PREFERRED TONE: Gentle, grounding, validating; Avoid terms like 'mutation' or 'hallucination'."
VEY
(Nodding, satisfied) Excellent, Mendez. Discredit the footage, the journalists, anyone linking this to Aurelion. And Kwan, escalate forensic audit on LAMI. I want to know who put this Trojan horse in our system.
KWAN
Yes, Dr. Vey. We'll be beyond reproach.
Mendez begins typing furiously on his tablet, already drafting press releases. Iqbal stares at the screen, then at her own hands, flexing her fingers almost imperceptibly.
CUT TO:
19:30 BST - Hackney Wick
INT. NAILA'S FLAT - LIVING ROOM - EVENING
Naila sits hunched over her laptop, scrolling through news articles. Her apartment is dim, the only light from the screen reflecting in her tired eyes. She sees the new headlines, the softened language. "Neurostructural Mythos Disorder," "Resonance Adaptation."
She glances at the kitchen sink. The small, ribbed object is still there, undisturbed. It doesn't hum now, but Naila can *feel* its presence, a low thrum against her bones, a silent insistence.
She touches her jaw. Her teeth ache, not with pain, but with a strange, internal pressure. A feeling of *readiness*.
She tries to remember the taste of blood in her mouth this morning. The flexible teeth. The scream that wasn't a scream. The object in the sink. The dog...
She tries to forget it. But the hum is everywhere now. It's not just sound. It's... understanding.
Her eyes drift to the broken jar in the garden. She knows, deep down, that the object isn't inert. It's waiting.
A sudden, sharp THUMM. Her entire body shivers. She knows what it means. It's not a question. It's a statement.
FADE TO BLACK.
