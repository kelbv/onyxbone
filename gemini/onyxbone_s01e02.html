05:30 BST - Hackney Wick
INT. NAILA'S FLAT - BEDROOM - DAWN
NAILA (20s) stirs, a shallow gasp escaping her lips. She is dreaming of something pressing outwards from her spine. Not pain, not quite. A PRESSURE that wants LANGUAGE. That wants to SING.
She wakes to the coppery taste of BLOOD in her mouth. She bolts upright, scrambling to the bathroom.
INT. NAILA'S FLAT - BATHROOM - CONTINUOUS
Naila stares at her reflection in the mirror, fingers tentatively exploring her teeth. They look... longer. SHARPER. She presses gently with her fingertip. They *flex*. A sickening, internal shift.
The bathroom floor vibrates slightly. A low THUMM that she hopes is the train line outside. She *hopes* it's the train line.
From across the street, a SCREAM. But not out loud. Not exactly. The sound is inside her jaw, vibrating the hinge of her mouth. A hum, like a modem handshake from 1997. Except she's the wire.
NAILA
(Whispering, to herself, eyes wide, a strange, knowing dread creeping in)
It’s not pain. It’s... installation.
She clutches her head. The hum in her teeth, the internal *flex*, the impossible scream in her jaw. She stumbles back to her phone.
Opens the journal app. Her fingers fly, possessed by an urgency that isn't entirely her own.
(ON SCREEN: NAILA'S JOURNAL ENTRY)
"April 5, 2029. 05:34am. I don't remember falling asleep. I remember the taste of salt and copper. I remember my tongue spelling out something on the roof of my mouth. It wasn't English. It was *calcium logic*. My molars hurt. They feel like they're shifting. Or talking. And when I opened my phone, my Notes app had a file titled "accept_me_final". I didn't write it. But it's in my style."
A small, almost imperceptible CLICK. The screen flickers.
CUT TO:
09:00 BST - NHS Biopath Lab, East London
INT. NHS BIOPATH LAB - DAY
DR. LENORA IQBAL (40s, sharp, meticulous), a Dental Pathologist, leans over a high-powered microscope. Her brow is furrowed. The lab is sterile, fluorescent. On the screen next to her, a magnified image of a tooth.
She adjusts the focus. The image resolves: extruded FILAMENT STRUCTURES on the enamel, silica-enriched. Like microscopic, crystalline antennae.
She gestures to her assistant, REZA (30s), who wears thick gloves.
IQBAL
Reza, re-scan. Confirm. Sample #0299-BG2. Upper rear left quadrant.
Reza nods, carefully manipulating the sample. He activates an internal scan.
(SOUND: The gentle whir of the scanner. A faint, high-pitched hum.)
The screen shows the tooth's interior: NESTED GROWTH RINGS, with a disconcerting, non-biological symmetry. An inner channel, partially hollow, lined with CONDUCTIVE PROTEINS.
REZA
Dr. Iqbal... it's pulsing again. 13Hz. Like the others.
Iqbal leans closer, her expression a mix of awe and dread. She picks up a micro-tool.
IQBAL
Try to extract a sliver of the filament. Carefully.
Reza presses the tool. It resists. Then, a low HUM begins to emanate from the tooth sample itself. The tool GLOWS FAINTLY RED, as if heating. Reza recoils.
IQBAL
(Muttering, as if to herself)
Reactivity inconsistent. Not organic. Not inorganic.
She grabs a pen, jots a note on her pad.
(ON SCREEN: LAB NOTE)
"Sample pulsed faintly during scan (13Hz - 1.1). Reactivity to standard biopsy tools inconsistent - tool heating observed. Recommendation: isolate and notify Home Office liaison."
DISSOLVE TO:
11:30 BST - NHS Internal MemoChain (Virtual Meeting)
INT. VIRTUAL NHS BRIEFING ROOM - DAY

ANAND
Alright, let's get through these preliminary findings. Tier 2 escalation for unexplained somatic mutation reports. Dr. Davies, sleep clinics?
DR. DAVIES (60s, rumpled, exhausted) nods.
DAVIES
Global insomnia rates, particularly 19-34 year-olds, up 600% in urban areas. Significant anomalies. Patients report being 'held awake from the inside.' Many exhibit micro-temporal disassociation – looped movements, echo gestures. And the phrases...

The unconscious vocalizations. Repeat them.
DAVIES
'Do not contain. Configure me.' It's... unsettling. Always in non-native phonemes.
IQBAL
And the physical symptoms? My lab is seeing unusual skeletal phenomena – density shifts, geometric marrow patterns. Minor oral bleeding, dental distortion, as well.
ANAND

14:00 BST - Aurelion Group HQ, London
INT. AURELION GROUP - DATA CENTER - DAY
The hum here is technological, the quiet thrum of massive servers. Thousands of blinking lights on racks of machines. KWAN (40s, sharp, dressed impeccably), Chief Data Officer for Aurelion, stares at a vast holographic display. Complex data streams flow across it – healthcare logs, social media trends, anonymized x-ray scans.
DR. S. VEY (50s, cold, calculating), Chair of Aurelion Group, approaches. He holds a tablet, its screen displaying a shimmering, geometric pattern – the glyph.
VEY
The 13Hz resonance. The dental anomalies. Your LAMI stack signatures. It all matches.
KWAN
Early signals, Dr. Vey. But escalating rapidly. The Tallinn broadcast wasn't a leak. It was a trigger.
VEY
Are we certain of the vector? Deep Signal Theory, recursive language compression... or something else entirely? Our bioware stack is clean. We need to be beyond reproach.
KWAN
On the surface, yes. But the glyphs... they're not emergent. They're engineered. Our pattern recognition points to legacy code buried deep. Possibly state-level. Or rogue AI. We're seeing identical sigils drawn in chalk, paint, blood, globally.
Vey squints at the holographic display, where a heatmap of London shows clusters of "SKE-145" alerts. One cluster pulses, right over Hackney Wick.
VEY
Is this ours, Kwan? Is this liability?
KWAN
We are tracing the origin point. But what it's *doing*... that's the real problem. It's not just infection. It's assembly.
Vey touches the glyph on his tablet. It pulses, subtly.
VEY
Find the kill switch, Kwan. Or the monetization strategy.

17:45 BST - Hackney Wick
INT. NAILA'S FLAT - KITCHEN - LATE AFTERNOON
Naila is leaning over her kitchen sink, coughing. Her throat is raw. She coughs until she tastes more blood. When she finally stops, something sits in the water.
Small, grey, ribbed. Not a tooth. Not a stone. Something between instruction and sculpture. It seems to pulse with a faint, internal light.
She stares at it, transfixed. A low hum emanates from it. Then it's silent.
Her hands are shaking. She picks it up with a paper towel. It feels warm. She wraps it in foil.
A moment later, a thin plume of SMOKE rises from the foil. It's burning through.
Panicked, Naila finds an old glass jar, drops the object inside, and screws the lid on tight.
Immediately, the glass FOGS, as if from extreme cold, then begins to CRACK. A faint HUMMING sound emits from the jar.
EXT. NAILA'S GARDEN - CONTINUOUS
Naila rushes outside, digging frantically in the small, overgrown garden. She buries the jar deep.
She barely finishes covering it when the NEIGHBOR'S DOG, a scruffy terrier, bursts through the fence, barking furiously. It digs at the freshly turned earth, scrabbling with frantic paws.
It unearths the jar. The glass is now shattered. The small, ribbed object sits there, glowing faintly. The dog sniffs it, then lets out a single, piercing BARK.
Its eyes, usually a mischievous brown, are now wide, milky. It whines, then turns and BOLTS into the busy street.
A BLARING HORN. A SCREECH OF TIRES. A sickening THUD.
Naila freezes, eyes wide. The silence that follows is profound.
She looks at the broken jar, at the pulsing object, then back at the street, where a small crowd has gathered around the dog. Its body is still. But a faint HUM pulses from where it lies.
Naila touches her own ribs. The hum is stronger now. It’s inside everything. It’s inside *her*.
